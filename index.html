Privacy Policy

Effective Date: 1st July 2026

Last Updated: 1st July 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how LaunchGirls (“LaunchGirls”, “we”, “our”, or “us”) collects, uses, stores, shares, and protects personal information when you visit launchgirls.org, contact us, participate in our programs, events, initiatives, communities, or communicate with us through channels such as email, phone, forms, social media, or WhatsApp.

By using our website, submitting information to us, participating in our programs, or communicating with us, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy.

1. About LaunchGirls

LaunchGirls is an organisation focused on supporting, enabling, and empowering girls and women through programs, learning opportunities, mentoring, community engagement, events, resources, and related initiatives.

This Privacy Policy applies to information collected through our website and other official communication channels operated by LaunchGirls.

2. Information We Collect

We may collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us, including:

  • Name
  • Email address
  • Phone number
  • City, state, or country
  • Age group or educational/professional background, where relevant to a program
  • Organisation, school, college, or workplace details, where applicable
  • Information submitted through forms, registrations, surveys, applications, or contact requests
  • Messages, questions, feedback, or other communication you send to us
  • Program participation details, attendance information, and preferences

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for purposes including:

  • Responding to your questions, enquiries, or requests
  • Processing registrations for programs, events, workshops, or initiatives
  • Communicating updates about LaunchGirls programs, opportunities, and activities
  • Providing support, guidance, resources, or mentoring-related communication
  • Managing participation in communities, programs, or events
  • Improving our website, programs, services, and outreach
  • Sending reminders, confirmations, follow-ups, and important notices
  • Maintaining records for operational, legal, reporting, or compliance purposes
  • Preventing misuse, fraud, spam, or unauthorised activity

4. WhatsApp Communications

If you contact LaunchGirls through WhatsApp or opt in to receive WhatsApp messages from us, we may collect and process your name, phone number, message content, communication history, and related information necessary to respond to you or provide relevant updates.

We may use WhatsApp to:

  • Respond to enquiries and support requests
  • Send program, event, or registration updates
  • Share reminders, confirmations, and relevant announcements
  • Provide information about LaunchGirls initiatives you have shown interest in
  • Communicate with volunteers, mentors, partners, applicants, or participants

We will only send WhatsApp communications where we have a lawful basis to do so, such as your consent, your request for information, your participation in a program, or another legitimate organisational purpose.

You may opt out of WhatsApp communications at any time by replying with “STOP” or by contacting us using the details provided in this policy.

5. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our website may use cookies or similar technologies to improve website functionality, understand visitor usage, and enhance user experience.

Cookies are small files stored on your device. You may disable cookies through your browser settings. However, some parts of the website may not function properly if cookies are disabled.

6. Legal Basis for Processing

Depending on the context, we may process your personal information based on:

  • Your consent
  • The need to respond to your request or provide a service you asked for
  • Our legitimate interest in operating and improving LaunchGirls programs
  • Compliance with legal, regulatory, reporting, or organisational obligations
  • Protection of rights, safety, and security of LaunchGirls, participants, and the public

7. Sharing of Information

We do not sell your personal information.

We may share personal information only where necessary with:

  • Authorised LaunchGirls team members, volunteers, mentors, or representatives
  • Service providers who help us operate our website, communication systems, forms, email, WhatsApp messaging, analytics, or event management
  • Partner organisations involved in specific programs or initiatives, where relevant and appropriate
  • Legal, regulatory, or government authorities where required by law
  • Professional advisers, auditors, or consultants where necessary for organisational operations

Service providers are expected to handle personal information securely and only for the purposes for which it was shared.

8. Data Retention

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary for the purposes described in this Privacy Policy, including program administration, communication, reporting, legal, accounting, or compliance requirements.

When information is no longer required, we may delete, anonymise, or securely archive it.

9. Data Security

We take reasonable organisational, technical, and administrative measures to protect personal information against unauthorised access, misuse, loss, alteration, or disclosure.

However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is completely secure. While we strive to protect your information, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

10. Your Rights and Choices

Subject to applicable law, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal information we hold about you
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information
  • Request deletion of your personal information
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent
  • Object to or restrict certain processing activities
  • Opt out of promotional or informational communications

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the details provided below.

11. Children and Young Participants

LaunchGirls may run or support programs intended for girls, students, or young participants. Where required by applicable law, we may seek consent from a parent, guardian, school, institution, or authorised representative before collecting or using personal information from minors.

If you believe that a child has provided personal information to us without appropriate consent, please contact us so that we can review and take appropriate action.

12. Third-Party Links

Our website or communications may contain links to third-party websites, platforms, forms, social media pages, or services. We are not responsible for the privacy practices, content, or security of those third-party services.

We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party websites or services you visit.

13. International Data Transfers

Depending on the tools, platforms, or service providers we use, your information may be processed or stored in countries other than your country of residence. Where applicable, we take reasonable steps to ensure appropriate safeguards are in place for such transfers.

14. Updates to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, programs, services, legal requirements, or operational needs.

The updated version will be posted on this page with a revised “Last Updated” date. We encourage you to review this page periodically.
